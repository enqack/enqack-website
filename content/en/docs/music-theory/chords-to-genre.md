## Creative heuristics

- Use inversions, omit the 5th, or add 7ths/9ths to make a familiar progression feel fresh without altering root movement too much.
- Use modal interchange: borrow chords from parallel modes (e.g. in C major, borrow A♭, B♭, etc. from C minor) to add surprise.
- Use suspended chords (sus2, sus4) and add9, 7, 11 to give air and tension.
- Use vamping on a section (stick on one chord) but modulate color via texture, rhythmic displacement, voicing drift.
- Insert passing chords lightly — e.g. between C and Dm, insert C♯°, or between Am and F insert E7sus4, etc.

## Cross-genre twists

- Start with a synthwave progression (Am – F – C – G) but in the bridge shift to a chillwave take (Fmaj7 – G – Am7 – Em) to soften, then back.
- Use a techno loop (Am – F – G) and overlay a chord “splash” (e.g. Dm7 or B♭maj7) every 8 bars.
- Borrow a jazz chord (e.g. G13, Dm9) inside a trance progression as a pivot chord to change texture.
- Use a chromatic mediant substitution: e.g. in C → Am → F → G progression, replace F with A♭ or Fm for a brief dramatic color, then resolve back to C.
